Voltaire

French writer and philosopher

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1694 - born in Paris, France.
  • 1709 - he attended the Louis-le-Grand College, where he studied with the Jesuits.
  • 1713 - he obtained the position of secretary of the French embassy in The Hague, a job from which he was expelled due to certain love relationships.
  • 1718 - he definitively adopted the pseudonym Voltaire (anagram of árouet le Jeune or of his father's place of origin, Air-vault).
  • 1720 - he premiered the play Oedipus at the Théâtre-Français, which was very well received.
  • 1724 - he premiered the play Mariana.
  • 1728 - he published the work Henriade and it achieved great success, both in France and in the rest of the European continent.
  • 1731 - he wrote Historia de Carlos XII, a work in which he outlines the problems and topics that, later, will appear fully matured in his famous work Philosophical Letters.
  • 1746 - he was elected a member of the French Academy.
  • 1750 - he moved to Germany and during his stay in Berlin, The Century of Louis XIV completed a historical study of the reign of that monarch.
  • 1756 - he bought a property in Ferney, where he spent the rest of his life. During this period he completed the Essay on General History and on the Customs and Character of Nations.
  • 1757- 1775 - he wrote several philosophical poems, such as The Lisbon Disaster, several satirical and philosophical novels.
  • 1778 - dies at the age of 83.
